# Summary — Coulomb Interaction-Driven Entanglement of Electrons on Helium

**Beysengulov, Schøyen, Bilek, Flaten, Leinonen, Hjorth-Jensen, Pollanen, Kristiansen, Stewart,
Weidman, Wilson.** PRX Quantum **5**, 030324 (2024). BibTeX key: `beysengulov2024`.
PDF: `../2024_Beysengulov_coulomb-entanglement_PRXQ5.pdf`.

## Thesis
Theoretically investigate **motional entanglement between two electrons on helium generated by their
unscreened Coulomb interaction**, and develop the numerical machinery (Hartree + full CI) to compute
the entangled states and optimize the device for two-qubit operation.

## Model & method
- Two electrons in a seven-electrode double-well; dimensionless Hamiltonian
  `Ĥ = Σ_i[−½ d²/dx_i² + v(x_i)] + u(x₁,x₂)`, `v(x) = −Σ_k α_k(x)V_k`, soft Coulomb
  `u = κ/√((x₁−x₂)²+ε²)`, `κ = 2326`, `ε = 1e-2`, energy unit `E_d = ℏ²/mₑx₀²`, `x₀=123 nm`.
- Deep wells ⇒ **distinguishable particles**; expand in a **product basis** (validated against
  antisymmetrized fermionic CI). Single-particle **sinc-DVR** (Colbert–Miller), grid cut at the
  barrier to confine each electron to its well.
- **Hartree method for distinguishable particles** builds compact single-particle bases that absorb
  the Coulomb mean field; **full CI** then diagonalizes `|Ψ_n⟩ = Σ C_{ij,n}|φ^L_iφ^R_j⟩`.
- Entanglement via **von Neumann entropy** (base-2), computed from the **Schmidt/SVD** of `C_{ij,n}`;
  the Hartree basis is an approximate common Schmidt basis.

## Configurations (the organizing result)
- **I (idle):** distinct L/R frequencies, `Δ_I = ω_L−ω_R > 0`, correlations minimized (entropy≈0);
  eigenstates 0,1,2,4 ↔ `|00⟩,|01⟩,|10⟩,|11⟩` are the computational basis.
- **II (iSWAP-type):** avoided crossing of first two excited states (`Δ_II=0`);
  `|1⟩=(|10⟩−|01⟩)/√2`, `|2⟩=(|10⟩+|01⟩)/√2`, entropy 1.
- **III (CZ-type):** triple avoided crossing among `{|20⟩,|11⟩,|02⟩}` (`Δ_III=−Δ_I/2`), entropies
  `{1.5,1,1.5}`.
- **Opposite-sign anharmonicities** `β_L=−β_R=Δ_I/2` separate the two crossings and suppress ZZ.
  Detunings in I and III have opposite sign, guaranteeing a config-II crossing along the interpolation
  `V(λ)=(1−λ)V_I+λV_III`.

## Effective model & ZZ
Bogoliubov/coupled-oscillator effective Hamiltonian reproduces the coupling `g` and gives
`ζ = E₄−E₂−E₁+E₀ ≈ 2g²/(Δ+β_L) − 2g²/(Δ−β_R)`, vanishing for opposite-sign equal-magnitude
anharmonicities in the harmonic limit; CI shows deviations (nonlinearities), motivating direct `ζ`
minimization. Coupling at the config-II crossing `g_CI ≈ 113 MHz`.

## Appendices (useful implementation detail)
Electron–ripplon coupling; sinc-DVR construction and matrix elements; the Hartree method derivation;
full CI for distinguishable particles; von Neumann entropy via Schmidt decomposition; particle
densities; cost functions for the configurational search; optimized electrode voltage tables.
