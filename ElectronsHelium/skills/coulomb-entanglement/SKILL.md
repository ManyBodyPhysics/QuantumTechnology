# Coulomb interaction-driven entanglement (PRX Quantum 5, 030324 (2024))

Backbone theory: how two electrons on helium become entangled through Coulomb interaction, and how
the many-body states are computed. The gate work (`two-qubit-gates`) and the sensor states
(`quantum-sensing`) both start from the eigenstates produced here. For the numerical *recipes* (grids,
matrix elements, diagonalization), read `ci-dvr-hartree-numerics`; this skill covers the physics and
the structure of the calculation.

## Model

Two electrons in a shaped (double-well) potential, dimensionless units (`ℏ²/2mₑ = 1`):

```
Ĥ = Σ_i [ −½ d²/dx_i² + v(x_i) ]  +  u(x₁,x₂)
```

- `v(x) = −Σ_k α_k(x) V_k ≡ −α(x)·V` — electrostatic potential from the gate voltages `V_k`;
  `α_k(x)` (dimensionless) are electrode form factors from a finite-element solve.
- `u(x₁,x₂) = κ / sqrt((x₁−x₂)² + ε²)` — **soft (regularized) Coulomb**, `ε = 0.01` removes the
  singularity; strength `κ = 2326` in the papers' energy unit (`E_d = ℏ²/m_e x₀²`, `x₀ = 123 nm`).
- The interaction is treated **unscreened** (upper bound on coupling); screening by the electrodes is
  a correction handled separately (see `two-qubit-gates`, screening section).

The 1D reduction is justified when the `y` axis is ~6× stiffer; the sensing paper generalizes the
same construction to full 2D. Because deep wells suppress interdot tunnelling, the electrons are
effectively **distinguishable**, so the many-body expansion uses **product states, not Slater
determinants** (validated against antisymmetrized full CI — results match).

## Method (Hartree → CI)

1. **Single-particle basis** per well via **sinc-DVR** (Colbert–Miller); restrict each electron to
   its own well by cutting the grid at the barrier `x_b` (an effective infinite wall). Kinetic and
   (diagonal) potential/Coulomb matrix elements follow closed forms — see `ci-dvr-hartree-numerics`.
2. **Hartree method for distinguishable particles** — solve the two coupled mean-field eigenvalue
   problems self-consistently to get compact single-particle bases `{|φ^A_i⟩}` (A ∈ {L,R}) that
   already absorb most of the Coulomb physics. This is what lets a *small* basis converge.
3. **CI (exact diagonalization)** — expand `|Ψ_n⟩ = Σ_{ij} C_{ij,n} |φ^L_i φ^R_j⟩` and diagonalize.
   Each column is a two-body eigenstate with energy `E_n`.

## Reading the states

- **Von Neumann entropy** `S = −tr[ρ̂ log₂ ρ̂]` of a subsystem quantifies entanglement per
  eigenstate (`S_n`). Compute it cheaply from the **Schmidt decomposition** (SVD of `C_{ij,n}`); the
  Hartree basis serves as an approximate common Schmidt basis, so each eigenstate is dominated by one
  or two product terms.
- **Natural / dominant orbitals** (largest-eigenvalue blocks of the spin-summed one-body density
  matrix restricted to L and R) define the *best* left/right single-orbital description of a
  correlated CI state — used later to build the reference singlet/triplet for sensing.

## The three configurations (the organizing idea)

Named well shapes, reached by tuning `V(λ) = (1−λ)V_I + λV_III` (config II appears en route):

- **Configuration I — idle.** Distinct L/R transition frequencies (detuning `Δ_I = ω_L − ω_R > 0`),
  correlations minimized, entropy ≈ 0 for all eigenstates. The four eigenstates with maximum overlap
  on `|00⟩,|01⟩,|10⟩,|11⟩` are the computational basis. Single-qubit control/readout happen here.
- **Configuration II — iSWAP-type.** An **avoided crossing** of the first two excited states at
  degeneracy (`Δ_II = 0`), giving `|1⟩=(|10⟩−|01⟩)/√2`, `|2⟩=(|10⟩+|01⟩)/√2` with entropy 1.
- **Configuration III — CZ-type.** A **triple avoided crossing** among `{|20⟩,|11⟩,|02⟩}`
  (`Δ_III = −Δ_I/2`), entropies `{1.5, 1, 1.5}`. The conditional shift here builds the CZ phase.

The **opposite-sign anharmonicity** trick (`β_L = −β_R = Δ_I/2`) keeps the two-excited-state avoided
crossing separated from the triple crossing and helps suppress unwanted ZZ. Detunings in I and III
have opposite sign *by design*, which guarantees a config-II crossing somewhere on the interpolation
path.

## ZZ coupling

`ζ = E₄ − E₂ − E₁ + E₀` — the shift in one qubit's transition when the other is excited; encodes
coupling to higher states. Small `ζ` is wanted in I/II (phase-error suppression); the config-III
shift is *used* to make the CZ. An effective-Hamiltonian (Bogoliubov) treatment gives
`ζ ≈ 2g²/(Δ+β_L) − 2g²/(Δ−β_R)`, which vanishes for equal-magnitude opposite-sign anharmonicities in
the harmonic limit; CI shows deviations away from that limit (nonlinearities), motivating direct
numerical minimization of `ζ`.

## What downstream skills take from here

- `two-qubit-gates`: uses configs II/III + time-dependent `V(λ(t))` to drive √iSWAP / CZ.
- `quantum-sensing`: uses the config-III–region singlet-like ground state and triplet-like excited
  state (built from the dominant natural orbitals) as the sensing two-level system.
